Abstract

With the rapid development of society, the output of waste has increased exponentially. To offset this the world has been attempting to move towards a circular society in terms of resources. Recycling is a large aspect of creating this circular society, however, currently, most recycling is too contaminated with rubbish and therefore ends up in landfills. A project is proposed to use machine learning techniques to detect contaminated recycling and use the data to help reduce the amount of contamination. This paper reviews current literature and technology relating to automatic detection and identification of rubbish during collection and sorting.
